# 🎯 Summary of What I Have Gained from This Course

The Technology and Information System (TIS) course provided a comprehensive introduction to the fundamental concepts of information technology and information systems. Throughout the semester, I gained knowledge of computer hardware, software, databases, networking, cybersecurity, and the role of information systems in organizations and society.

The course combined theoretical learning with practical activities that allowed me to apply technology concepts to real-world situations. Through assignments, reports, presentations, and hands-on activities, I developed a better understanding of how technology supports communication, business operations, and decision-making processes.

### Key Learning Activities

#### 🖥️ PC Assembly

The PC assembly activity provided hands-on experience in identifying computer components and understanding how hardware components work together within a computer system.

#### 🎨 Design Thinking Project

The Design Thinking Report and Presentation introduced a structured approach to problem-solving by focusing on user needs, creativity, innovation, and solution development. This activity strengthened my ability to analyze problems and develop technology-based solutions.

#### 📢 Poster, Video, Academic Report, and Newsletter

These assignments improved my ability to research, analyze information, and communicate ideas effectively through various formats, including visual presentations, written reports, videos, and newsletters.

#### 📁 Portfolio and Reflection

The portfolio and reflection activities encouraged continuous learning and self-assessment, allowing me to document my progress and reflect on the knowledge and skills gained throughout the course.

### Key Learning Outcomes

- Fundamentals of information systems
- Computer hardware and software concepts
- Networking and database basics
- Technology problem-solving
- Design thinking methodology
- Research and analytical skills
- Communication and presentation skills
- Teamwork and collaboration
- Digital literacy

## 🌟 Personal Reflection

One of the most valuable aspects of this course was the opportunity to connect theoretical knowledge with practical applications. The design thinking project taught me how to analyze problems from different perspectives and develop user-centered technological solutions. Meanwhile, the PC assembly activity provided practical exposure to computer hardware that enhanced my understanding of how computer systems function.

The various assignments also improved my communication, teamwork, and presentation skills while encouraging creativity and critical thinking. These experiences helped me develop confidence in using technology to solve problems and communicate ideas effectively.

Overall, this course provided a strong foundation in technology and information systems while enhancing both my technical knowledge and professional skills.
