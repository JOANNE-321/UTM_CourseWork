Special Topic in Data Engineering (SECP3843) provided me with valuable exposure to modern data engineering technologies, industry practices, and emerging trends in big data processing. Through this assignment, I developed a deeper understanding of two major data processing approaches:

Although the course was highly beneficial, several improvements could further enhance the learning experience.

# 🌟 C. Personal Reflection

This course has significantly strengthened my interest in the field of data engineering.

Before taking this course, I mainly viewed data engineering as a technical discipline focused on coding, databases, and data pipelines. However, throughout the semester, I realized that data engineering serves a much larger purpose—it enables organizations to make informed, data-driven decisions.

The combination of academic learning, practical tutorials, and the industry-based PPG project allowed me to bridge the gap between theory and real-world applications. In addition to technical knowledge, I improved several soft skills, including:

* Problem-solving
* Communication
* Team collaboration
* Project management
* Analytical thinking

One of the most important lessons I learned is that successful data engineering is not solely about building pipelines and processing data. It also requires understanding business requirements and delivering meaningful insights that create value for organizations.

This realization has motivated me to further develop my expertise in both **Data Engineering** and **Business Analytics**, as I believe the combination of technical and business knowledge is essential for future data professionals.

## 🚀 Conclusion

Overall, Special Topic in Data Engineering has been one of the most practical and impactful courses in my degree programme. The knowledge, technical skills, and industry exposure gained throughout this semester will serve as a strong foundation for my future career in the data industry.
